# Xenia — Landing Page Storyboard

*The cinematic blueprint for the marketing website (Evolution Plan Phase 5).
Not implementation, not mockups: the film the page plays as a visitor
scrolls. Constitutional guardrails, stated once and binding on every
chapter: **every animated moment depicts the product's actual mechanics**
(Doc 11 §2 — scroll animation may only explain), motion settles and never
performs (M10), copy obeys the banned register (Doc 06 §2), nothing shown
is fabricated — demo data is canned but structurally real, no invented
metrics, no fake testimonials (M9). With `prefers-reduced-motion`, the
entire page must read perfectly as a still, beautifully set document: the
film is an enhancement of the story, never its carrier. One continuous
camera; no scene ever cuts — each hands its objects to the next.*

**The visual world:** a single dark, warm space — think reading-room at
night, not server-room. Materials: paper (documents, cards), thread
(light-lines connecting evidence), and glass (the observation pane —
Xenia's lens on the world; the one sanctioned glass metaphor). Light is
the story's protagonist: it begins scattered and ends settled.

---

## Chapter 1 — Hidden Operational Chaos

- **Purpose:** name the founder's real Monday — prospecting as scattered,
  manual, joyless work — without insulting them for it.
- **Emotion:** recognition. *"That is my Saturday morning."*
- **Narrative:** somewhere out there are the ten businesses that should be
  your clients this quarter. They are buried in noise you'd have to dig
  through by hand.
- **Camera:** slow drift forward through a dark field, slightly below eye
  line, as if walking into a paper archive at night.
- **3D environment:** hundreds of faint paper fragments suspended in
  space — job posts, filings, ad snippets, half-readable — drifting
  slowly, unaligned, at every depth.
- **Motion:** fragments drift on independent slow orbits; a few catch
  brief legibility as the camera passes. Scroll advances the walk; nothing
  else responds to the visitor yet.
- **Glass:** none. The world before Xenia has no lens.
- **Lighting:** near-dark; cold scattered glints off fragment edges; the
  warm paper tone exists only in embers.
- **Copy:** **"Your next ten clients are already out there."** /
  *Somewhere in the filings, the job ads, the site changes. Finding them
  by hand is a weekend job that never ends.*
- **Transition:** one fragment directly ahead brightens faintly — a hiring
  post — and the camera commits toward it as the first thread of light
  touches it.

## Chapter 2 — Signals Begin Connecting

- **Purpose:** introduce the first mechanic: observation becomes signal.
- **Emotion:** curiosity; the first sense of order.
- **Narrative:** Xenia reads the public world politely and notices what a
  practised analyst notices — this company is hiring its first marketer;
  that one just started running ads.
- **Camera:** settles to eye line; begins a slow lateral track, following
  threads as they form.
- **3D environment:** the same field, but warm threads of light begin
  linking fragments that belong to the same business — three or four
  clusters condensing out of the noise.
- **Motion:** scroll-linked: each increment draws one thread from fragment
  to fragment with the settle curve; touched fragments square up, align,
  and take the paper tone. Labels fade in beside clusters: *hiring ·
  running ads · filed accounts*.
- **Glass:** the first pane appears at frame edge — a narrow observation
  strip through which one cluster is seen slightly clarified: Xenia's
  lens, introduced before Xenia is named.
- **Lighting:** warm light travels *along* the threads, as if attention
  itself illuminates; the ambient dark lifts one stop.
- **Copy:** **"Xenia watches the signals that matter."** / *Companies
  House, hiring pages, ad libraries, websites — read politely, on a
  schedule, so you never have to.*
- **Transition:** the camera pushes through the glass strip; passing
  through it, the clusters sharpen — we now see the world as Xenia sees
  it.

## Chapter 3 — AI Discovers Relationships

- **Purpose:** show judgment forming — signals meeting *your* definition
  of a good client — without a single wire-brain cliché.
- **Emotion:** intelligence felt as calm competence, not magic.
- **Narrative:** signals alone are trivia. Against your Ideal Client DNA —
  your words, from a ten-minute interview — they become meaning.
- **Camera:** slow orbit around one cluster as a second object floats in:
  a small typeset document — the DNA — its statements legible ("DTC
  brands, £1–20m", "No franchises").
- **3D environment:** cluster left, DNA document right; threads reach from
  each DNA statement toward matching signals and take hold.
- **Motion:** each DNA line, on scroll, extends a thread that finds its
  signal and settles taut; one thread reaches a cluster and *stops short* —
  the franchise cluster dims and drifts away, respectfully. The first
  refusal, animated.
- **Glass:** the DNA document sits on a subtle glass sheet — co-owned,
  handled with care; the one artefact displayed under glass.
- **Lighting:** matched pairs glow warm; the dimmed cluster cools to
  grey — light now encodes fit.
- **Copy:** **"Judged against your definition of a good client — in your
  own words."** / *A ten-minute interview becomes your Ideal Client DNA.
  Every match is measured against it. Your hard lines are never crossed.*
- **Transition:** the matched cluster and its threads contract gently into
  a neat stack of citations, and the camera tilts down to follow the
  stack as it becomes a page.

## Chapter 4 — Evidence Before Recommendation

- **Purpose:** the receipt rule — the product's soul — given the page's
  most deliberate scene.
- **Emotion:** trust, produced structurally. *"I could check every line."*
- **Narrative:** before Xenia says a word about a business, the evidence
  is already numbered. Claims without receipts don't survive.
- **Camera:** overhead, descending slowly onto the assembling page — the
  reading angle; the most intimate move on the page.
- **3D environment:** a receipt table assembling line by line: [1], [2],
  [3] — each row arriving as a thread from the cluster delivers its
  fragment, which flattens into a printed line.
- **Motion:** rows settle in sequence on scroll; one arriving claim has no
  thread — it hovers, finds nothing beneath it, and dissolves. The L0
  validator, shown, not explained. A sentence then composes itself above
  the table with its citation marks [1][3] lighting their rows.
- **Glass:** none — evidence is paper, not spectacle. The absence is the
  point.
- **Lighting:** the page casts its own reading light now; the surrounding
  dark becomes a quiet study rather than a void.
- **Copy:** **"No claim without a receipt."** / *Every material statement
  cites numbered evidence you can inspect. What Xenia couldn't verify, she
  tells you plainly.*
- **Transition:** the finished page folds up into a typeset brief and
  slides to rest beside something new arriving from the right: a card
  with a verdict.

## Chapter 5 — The Judgment

- **Purpose:** the accountable posture — a colleague who says *this one,
  now, because* — and the visible exclusion beside it.
- **Emotion:** relief. Someone finally *decided* something.
- **Narrative:** research becomes a recommendation with its reasons on
  its face — and one business ruled out, with the law that ruled it.
- **Camera:** pulls back and levels: the recommendation card front and
  centre at conversational distance, the brief tucked behind it.
- **3D environment:** one card, verdict-first: business name, the word
  *likely*, three reasons; below the fold of the scene, a smaller grey
  card: *"ruled out — franchise model, your disqualifier."*
- **Motion:** the three reasons print in sequence; the confidence word
  settles last, like a stamp without theatre. The exclusion card simply
  *is* — no animation; refusals don't perform.
- **Glass:** none.
- **Lighting:** fully warm now around the card; the first frame of the
  page that feels like daylight.
- **Copy:** **"This business. This week. Here's why."** / *A bounded
  weekly queue — around ten, never padded — each with its reasons, and at
  the end, what was ruled out and by which of your rules.*
- **Transition:** the card glides down-left as siblings arrive above and
  below it, ranks appearing — the queue assembling — while a browser
  chrome quietly draws itself around them.

## Chapter 6 — The Xenia Workspace Assembles

- **Purpose:** reveal that everything witnessed lives in one calm product.
- **Emotion:** *"I want to open this on Monday."*
- **Narrative:** the film's objects were never illustrations — they were
  the product. The workspace is where they live.
- **Camera:** eases back until the assembled interface fills the frame at
  a natural laptop distance, then locks; the cinematic camera retires
  here.
- **3D environment:** collapses to 2D deliberately — the real queue
  screen (the actual component, canned data), in the actual ink/paper
  tokens, dark theme continuous with the film's lighting.
- **Motion:** the queue's cards settle-in exactly as they do in the
  product (same 200ms, same curve — the site demonstrably shares the
  app's motion vocabulary); a correction is clicked and the named effect
  appears: *"Removes one from this week's queue."*
- **Glass:** the browser chrome bar only — the last pane, framing the
  product.
- **Lighting:** flat, honest UI light; the theatrical lighting has
  finished its work.
- **Copy:** **"Ten minutes on Monday. That's the workflow."** / *Open the
  queue, make your calls, teach her with one tap. She learns; next Monday
  is sharper.*
- **Transition:** the locked frame becomes genuinely interactive —
  scrolling now moves *inside* the demo pane.

## Chapter 7 — Product Demonstration

- **Purpose:** let the visitor touch it: the loop, hands-on, unfaked.
- **Emotion:** confidence through competence — nothing is hidden behind a
  video.
- **Narrative:** decline with a chip, watch the acknowledgment; open the
  brief, see the fold and the receipts; open the DNA, see the changelog.
- **Camera:** none — the visitor is the camera now. Static page furniture;
  a quiet chapter list at the side tracks their exploration.
- **3D environment:** retired. Real components, real tokens, canned
  workspace data clearly labelled *demonstration workspace*.
- **Motion:** only the product's own motion vocabulary, live.
- **Glass:** the demo sits in a single elevated glass sheet on the paper
  page — the sanctioned transient-surface role, holding the product like
  an exhibit case that opens.
- **Lighting:** page-native; both themes honoured (the demo follows the
  visitor's OS scheme — dark mode is first-class here as everywhere).
- **Copy:** **"Try the loop."** / *This is a demonstration workspace with
  real mechanics: decline something, correct something, read a brief.
  Nothing here is a video.*
- **Transition:** scrolling past the demo, the glass sheet closes and
  recedes; the page's paper background continues into a quieter, denser
  section — the tone shifts from show to disclosure.

## Chapter 8 — Security & Trust

- **Purpose:** the trust section that is true: architecture as promises.
- **Emotion:** the exhale of due diligence going well.
- **Narrative:** what Xenia may see, what she may never do, and what
  happens when you leave — stated as built facts, each expandable to its
  mechanism.
- **Camera:** static; the page is now a document (M9: trust is exhibited
  as checkability, not claimed).
- **3D environment:** none. Four typeset panels: *No claim without a
  receipt (the L0 validator) · Your data is walled per workspace (RLS,
  audited) · No mass outreach — there is no send button to misuse ·
  Leaving is a feature (full export, provable deletion).*
- **Motion:** panels settle-in on entry; each expands in place to one
  paragraph of mechanism. Nothing else.
- **Glass:** none — promises on paper.
- **Lighting:** even, documentary.
- **Copy:** **"Built like it will be audited."** / *Because it is — by
  you, any time: every recommendation decomposes to named evidence, every
  change to your DNA is logged, and departure takes your data with you.*
- **Transition:** the panels align into a single centred column that
  becomes the pricing card's silhouette.

## Chapter 9 — Pricing

- **Purpose:** one plan, plainly priced — billing simplicity as brand.
- **Emotion:** the absence of suspicion. Nothing to decode.
- **Narrative:** founding rate, everything included, leave whenever —
  priced like the employee she replaces a fraction of.
- **Camera:** static, centred.
- **3D environment:** none. One card on paper. No tiers, no toggle, no
  "most popular" badge — the pricing table's absence *is* the design.
- **Motion:** the card settles once. Numbers do not count up.
- **Glass:** none.
- **Lighting:** unchanged.
- **Copy:** **"One plan. Founding rate."** / *Everything you saw above.
  Weekly queue, briefs, corrections, the DNA, export, departure. Cancel
  any time — leaving is a feature, not a negotiation.*
- **Transition:** the card's button carries the accent; scrolling past
  it, all remaining light on the page gathers toward the final line.

## Chapter 10 — Final Call to Action

- **Purpose:** close on the employee, not the software.
- **Emotion:** quiet resolve; Monday could be different.
- **Narrative:** the whole film in one sentence, then one act.
- **Camera:** a single slow half-step forward — the first camera motion
  since Chapter 6, and the last: the film's bookend.
- **3D environment:** near-empty warm dark; one thread of light from
  Chapter 2 crosses the space behind the headline — the story's
  signature, at rest.
- **Motion:** headline settles; the thread completes its line and stops.
  The page ends still.
- **Glass:** none.
- **Lighting:** a warm pool around the words; edges fade to the
  reading-room dark of Chapter 1 — the loop closed visually.
- **Copy:** **"Hire the analyst who shows her work."** / *Xenia finds and
  researches your ideal clients, with receipts. Founding cohort — by
  conversation, not by form.* [One accent button: **Talk to us**]
- **Transition:** none. Endings should end.
